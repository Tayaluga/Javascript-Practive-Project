Economic Structures When it comes to economics, the Netherlands and United States operate on varying scales. The Netherlands boasts a highly advanced and open economy relying quite heavily on international trade, being one of the world’s premier exporters. Rotterdam’s port, among the busiest globally, plays a pivotal role in its market. The U.S., however, is the largest economy worldwide. It’s characterized by an immense and diverse industrial foundation. Technology titans such as Apple and Google, financial powerhouses like Wall Street, along with a huge consumer base define the U.S. economy. Despite its enormous size and impact, the U.S. market faces challenges, including income inequality and periodic financial crises.

Political Systems Politically, the Netherlands and U.S. adhere to differing structures. The Netherlands has a parliamentary democracy while the U.S. has a presidential system. Both aim to serve citizens through representation, yet their approaches diverge. The Netherlands parliament consists of elected members proportionally representing the multi-party system. In contrast, American politics involves electing a president not directly by popular vote but via an electoral college system involving the Senate and House of Representatives.
